How This Would Work in Reality

1. Instrument SPV

Each instrument sits in a special-purpose company that legally owns it. Investors own shares of that company, not the violin directly.

2. Fractional Shares

The SPV is split into a fixed number of shares. Investors buy fractions, similar to how you might own fractional shares of a stock.

3. Voting on Musicians

Shareholders vote on which musician receives a performance loan, with votes weighted by number of shares.

4. Income & Impact

In a full implementation, performance fees or appreciation could flow back to shareholders while instruments stay in the hands of artists.
